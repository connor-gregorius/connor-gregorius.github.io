Journey to Earth

Our adventure starts with the observable universe, here are some interesting facts and details about it:

  • We aren’t limited by our technology as to why we can only see what we see so far, basically it's just because any signals given out by anything outside of the observable universe couldn’t have reached us yet
  • It's diameter is roughly 93 billion light years in length
  • It's mass is roughly 10^50 tons
  • There are at least 2 trillion galaxies in the observable universe
  • All currently observable objects will eventually appear to freeze in time, while emitting progressively redder and fainter light due to the universe expanding
  • Light emitted by objects currently situated beyond a certain comoving distance (currently about 62 billion light years) will never reach Earth
  • Some objects in the universe will never be observable, as they are travelling away from Earth faster than the speed of light
  • it is estimated that the there are between 10^78 to 10^82 atoms in the known, observable universe
  • Though well known, the universe itself is constantly expanding, but what some may not know is that it's rate of growth (acceleration) is also increasing
  • 96% of the universe is made up of substances that cannot be seen or easily comprehended.
